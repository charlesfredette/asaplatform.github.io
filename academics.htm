School Overview

In 2017, Sainte-Anne was ranked 1st by the Fraser Institute for the elite secondary schools in Quebec. The College has also received numerous awards in education innovation. It is also part of the FEEP (Federation of Quebec private schools). The student body is ethnically diversified, and 90% of students live in the West Island of Montreal suburbs.

Curriculum

Collège Sainte-Anne's academic program is organized around a 7 day cycle schedule. Each day is divided into 6 class periods, including one for lunch. Classes start at 8:05am, lasts 1 hour each, with 10-minute breaks between. Classes end everyday at 3pm.

Most available classes at Sainte-Anne are enriched or have enriched options. Throughout the 5 years, French classes go beyond the requirements from the Quebec Education Ministry, allowing teachers to go more in depth with the class. Classes that offer enriched options are:

  • - English (Secondary 1 to 5)
  • - Mathematics (Secondary 4 & 5)
  • - Sciences - Chemistry & Physics (Secondary 5)
